  • What can your hobbies tell us that your resume can't?


    I believe that the home is a sacred space. Its where we go to relax, to recharge, it should be beautiful, it should soothe us. The things we keep should reflect that.

    There is something primal and satisfying to me about sitting down with your family for dinner at a table you build with your bare hands. Not just a table you put together, but a table you envisioned, designed yourself, sourced your own salvaged materials to build, and then went down to the garage, and built with your bare hands (plus a lot of sawdust and sweat).

    One of my biggest passions is woodworking. In my free time you can find me salvaging fallen trees, reclaiming old timbers, and kicking up sawdust and noise in my garage.

    My design style is a modern version of rustic industrial. I work exclusively with wood that I salvaged and reclaimed myself.

  • Describe your ideal work environment.


    I am a laid back intensity junkie. I bounce back and forth from extreme focus to chill and playful. This is my sweetspot.

    I've been told that Einstein would work on intense theories of physics and relativity and when faced with a massive challenge, instead of forcing the answer when he was stuck, he would simply go take a break, and go sailing.

    Often, while drifting along the shoreline the answer to his question would pop into his head, and he would turn back and finish his work.

    This resonates with me and my workstyle.

    My workstyle is best suited for an industry where results matter, but I can be free to work in my own way, to use my mind to challenge and influence the variables of the problem to create the best solution. The deadline will be met, because the client comes first, always. Sleep comes second.
